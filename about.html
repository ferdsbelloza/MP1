Silent Library

Throughout the 1900s, under the names Silent Town Library and Silent Reading Room and Library Association, a book-lending institution existed in such locations as the Third Congregational Church and on the fourth floor of what is now 115 Jubilee Ave. For a subscription fee, members could borrow an allotted number of books. Around 1920, a small collection of books was acquired and circulated from the Nebukadenar Opique House. In 1925, a small group of Jubilee residents, each subscribing $6 a share, formed the Silent Town Library.
